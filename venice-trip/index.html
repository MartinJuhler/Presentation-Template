💡 Practical Tips

Making Venice easy with kids aged 5 and 7

🎫
Transport Passes
  • Vaporetto tickets: A 7-day pass is €65/adult. Children under 6 ride free. Your 7-year-old needs a ticket. Single rides are €9.50, so a pass pays for itself quickly.
  • Alilaguna is a separate service — vaporetto passes do NOT work on Alilaguna.
  • Buy at veneziaunica.it or at any ACTV ticket office.
🌤️
Weather in Late February
  • Expect 5–10°C daytime, colder evenings. Dress in layers.
  • Rain is possible — bring waterproof jackets and shoes.
  • Acqua alta (high water) can happen. Check the tide forecast. If water rises, elevated walkways are set up at main routes. Consider packing foldable wellies for the kids!
👶
Venice with Kids
  • Stroller vs carrier: A lightweight umbrella stroller works in Castello (flat area), but a child carrier/backpack is better for exploring areas with many bridges.
  • Playgrounds: Giardini Napoleonici (near your suite!), Parco Savorgnan (near train station), and Burano island playground.
  • Snacks: Carry snacks and water — there are few shops in quiet alleyways. Bakeries open early for fresh pastries.
  • Pace yourselves: Don't over-schedule. The magic of Venice is wandering and getting happily lost.
