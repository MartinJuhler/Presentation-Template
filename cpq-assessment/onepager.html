What Was Done

After taking over the CPQ project, I first attempted to get the existing setup working. When that proved structurally unfixable (static data, no propagation, silent breakage), I challenged Infor to propose a new approach that could actually solve AKVA's use case. After 2 months of building momentum to even get the right people in the room, they proposed scrapping everything and starting over with 2-3 new matrices, and developing bespoke integrations to make it work. Even after a month of analysis to come up with a usable solution, the proposed approach would only cover standard items. Infor had no in-production references to point to, had done zero testing of a working solution, and - even knowing everything was at stake - could not commit to a timeline or cost estimate.

Why It Cannot Work for AKVA

Existing Setup

🔄 No Automatic Data Sync

Product data and options are 100% static. Any ERP change breaks CPQ silently. Tens of thousands of components require constant manual upkeep.

📂 Convoluted Interface

Myriad of interdependent matrices that reference each other - but updating one does not propagate. UI so convoluted that participants were highly confused. Requires coding knowledge to operate.

👥 One Developer at a Time

No branching, merging, or automated testing. Changes traced via manual comments only. Only one person can work on it at a time. Doesn't scale.

💥 No Safety Logic

No protection against cascading failures. Renamed or removed items in ERP produce wrong quotes or crashes - with zero warning to the user.

Proposed New Approach - Still Broken

🤷 Infor Can't Commit

Support is siloed - no single person understands the full flow. Their best proposal has no in-production references, no timeline, and no cost estimate.

🔮 Only Standard Products

Even the proposed new approach only covers standard items. Complex product families like flåte are not solvable in this architecture. Expansion would require costly manual effort with ongoing consultant dependency.
